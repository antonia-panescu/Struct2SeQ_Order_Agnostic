## Eval pipeline built (Apr 28 01:46 UTC)

Goal: produce a knitnet-format `evaluation_summary.json` for `best_policy_network.pt`
(the Episode 4 best, MCC-reward 0.821) on the same test set the AR baseline
was evaluated on.

### Reproducing the original Struct2SeQ 3-strategy protocol
- Original `Struct2SeQ/generate.py` uses 3 inference strategies × 128 samples each:
  - `generate_sequence_batched(p=0.05)` — ε-greedy, near-uniform among allowed bases
  - `generate_sequence_batched(p=0.10)` — same with higher ε
  - `generate_sequence_batched_sample(p=1.0)` — softmax(values) multinomial draw, true sampling
- This run uses **32 samples per strategy** (96 per target), and the **only deviation
  from the original protocol is decoding order**: per-sample random RNA-position
  permutation, matching this model's `--order-agnostic` training distribution
  rather than the original's left-to-right.

### Files
- `evaluation/run_eval.py` — single-GPU generator that produces `per_sample.csv`,
  `per_target.csv`, snapshots the checkpoint, captures stdout to `run.log`.
  Implements its own permutation-aware decoder (the existing
  `Functions.generate_sequence_batched` is L→R-only; KV-cache for transformer
  layers retained, but the LSTM positional encoding inside
  `OptimizedTransformerDecoder` is recomputed each step — that's an O(L²)
  property of the architecture, not avoidable without modifying the model).
- `evaluation/compute_metrics.py` — post-processes the CSVs into a knitnet-format
  JSON. **Imports knitnet's metric definitions verbatim** (`calculate_jaccard_index`,
  `calculate_structure_mcc`, `calculate_structure_accuracy`, `sequence_recovery`,
  `compute_nucleotide_metrics`) so numbers are directly comparable to existing
  knitnet eval JSONs (e.g. `outputs/evaluation/graphgps_AR_oracle_test_det/`).
- `run_eval.py` auto-calls `compute_metrics.py` at end of run.

**The ~10-point regression vs the untouched original (0.935 → 0.832) splits
into two independent contributions:**

### Contribution 1 — Inference-order mismatch costs ~8 points (architectural)

Same Struct2SeQ.pt weights, only the decoding order changes:
**L→R 0.935 → randperm 0.851** (-0.084 MCC).

The decoder's positional encoder is `nn.LSTM(embed, embed)` followed by a
`CausalConv1d(k=5)` (`Encoder_Decoder.py:208,213`). Both are intrinsically
left-to-right sequence operators. Under randperm decoding, the LSTM's
hidden state at step t encodes "I just saw the nucleotide at RNA position
`perm[t-1]`" rather than "I just saw the L→R prefix" — a non-sequitur for
an L→R-trained operator. Cross-attention to the encoder memory then has to
locate the current RNA position from content alone, since the only side
channel given to the decoder is the paired/unpaired indicator
`paired_encoding_perm` (no explicit RNA-position encoding for `perm[t]`).
That's not enough information; the model can't recover the L→R structural
prior under permutation.

### Contribution 2 — Catastrophic forgetting costs another ~10 points

Even when we evaluate the bidir-RL-trained checkpoint with **L→R**
inference (removing the order-mismatch confound), we get
**0.836 (ep0_epoch0) and 0.829 (ep0_epoch1)** — far below the
untouched 0.935 and barely above the original-with-randperm baseline of
0.851. So one epoch of permuted-data SGD has already washed out the
L→R-encoded structural prior in the original weights.

Across our four episodes the trajectory is **flat to slightly declining**
(0.840 → 0.840 → 0.830 → 0.832 — Ep1→Ep2→Ep3→Ep4). The model has converged
to a basin around 0.83–0.84, which is essentially "original-with-randperm"
performance: the RL training mostly recovers what permuted inference on the
untouched original already gave you, and never exceeds it. **More training
won't help with this architecture** — the curve has plateaued.

### Verdict

The user's hypothesis was correct: the LSTM positional encoder being L→R
**is** the foundational issue. Catastrophic forgetting from full-parameter
SGD at LR 1e-4 is the secondary issue stacked on top. "Didn't train enough"
is **not** a contributor — the curve is flat.

## Implications / next steps

In rough order of expected payoff:

1. **Replace the L→R positional encoder.** The decoder needs RNA-position-
   aware positional embeddings (sinusoidal/learned at `perm[t]`, not at
   step t), or AlphaFold-style relative-position cross-attention to the
   encoder. Drop the LSTM and the causal conv from the decoder — both bake
   in step-order inductive bias that conflicts with permuted decoding.
2. **Tell the decoder which RNA position it's predicting.** Currently the
   only side-channel is the paired/unpaired indicator. Add an explicit
   positional encoding for `perm[t]` so cross-attention has location info.
3. **If keeping current architecture:** much smaller LR + parameter-
   efficient finetuning (LoRA on attention only) to avoid blowing away the
   L→R prior. Or freeze encoder + decoder positional/conv layers, train
   only cross-attention + output head.
4. **Stop the running Episode-5 training.** The trajectory is flat across
   four episodes; another episode is not going to break the basin.
